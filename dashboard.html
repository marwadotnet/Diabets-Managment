Potential Complications & Side Effects

Understanding potential complications helps you recognize warning signs early and take preventive action

Immediate Effects

Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar)

Immediate attention needed

Symptoms:

  • Shakiness
  • Sweating
  • Confusion
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Dizziness

What to do:

Consume 15-20g of fast-acting carbs immediately. Recheck in 15 minutes.

Hyperglycemia (High Blood Sugar)

Monitor closely

Symptoms:

  • Increased thirst
  • Frequent urination
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Headache

What to do:

Stay hydrated, take medication as prescribed, and contact doctor if over 240 mg/dL.

Long-term Complications

While these complications can develop over time with poorly managed diabetes, they are largely preventable with proper care and monitoring.

Important

Most diabetes complications are preventable with proper management. Regular check-ups, consistent blood sugar monitoring, and following your healthcare provider's recommendations significantly reduce your risk. If you experience any concerning symptoms, contact your doctor immediately.
